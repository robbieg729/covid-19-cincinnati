Protecting yourself

The Ohio State Government has outlined the following guidance to help you better understand the virus in terms of symptoms, how to ensure you do not contract it, and what to do if you begin to feel sick. Please read through the following sections carefully and ensure that you and members of your household are following this guidance correctly.

In order to prevent yourself from contracting the virus, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommends that you follow these steps:

  • Wash your hands often with soap and water for 20 seconds, particularly after you have been in a public area, or after you have blown your nose, sneezed, or coughed

  • In a situation where soap and water is not available, hand sanitizer containing at least 60% alcohol is sufficient. You should cover your hands all over and rub them until they feel dry

  • When your hands are unwashed, avoid touching your face, specifically your eyes, nose, and mouth
  • Avoid close contact with people who exhibit symptoms of the virus, even if they are in your own home. If possible, try to maintain at least 6 feet distance between you and the person who is sick

  • Avoid close contact with anybody when outside your home

    • People who do not exhibit symptoms may still be able to transmit the virus

    • Ensure there is at least 6 feet between you and other people
  • Face masks are intended to prevent you from transmitting the virus to others, which could happen even if you do not feel sick or do not have symptoms

  • Face masks are not required in your own home, but are mandatory in all indoor public spaces in Cincinnati, and are highly recommended at all times when outside

    • It is not necessary to wear a face mask when sitting down at a bar or restaurant

  • Face masks should not be placed on children under the age of 2 or anyone who is having trouble breathing

  • NOTE: Face masks are not a substitute for social distancing, and this should still be practiced at all times when outside
  • If you are around others and not wearing a face mask, you should cover up a sneeze or a cough by placing a tissue over your mouth and nose

  • These tissues should then be thrown into the trash immediately, and you should then proceed to wash your hands in the advised manner (see 'Wash your hands regularly' section)
