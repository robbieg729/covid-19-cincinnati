What to do if you feel ill

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommends that you follow these steps if you begin to feel ill or contract the virus:

  • If your current symptoms match those common with Covid-19, you should book a test at the testing site nearest to you. You can go to the 'Interactive map' page in the menu on the left to look for testing sites near you

  • Most people who contract the virus will only have a mild fever and should be able to recover by resting at home. You should not leave your house unless it is to get medical care

  • The current CDC isolation period is 10 days after symptoms first appear, or 10 days after a positive test result if you received a test

  • You should stay in contact with your local doctor in case you feel there is an emergency such as severe shortness of breath. This is especially important for people who are more at-risk, such as those with underlying medical conditions

  • If you require assistance with shopping for groceries, the safest way to do so would be to call one of your neighbors who could go to the store for you and leave the items on your doorstep
  • When isolating, try to stay in a specific room and avoid other people and pets in your house as much as possible. If this is too difficult, wear a mask around others and practice social distancing

  • Where possible, you should use a separate bathroom to others in your house

  • Alert others that you have been in contact with recently that you may have contracted the virus, so that they know and can book a test
  • You should ensure that your symptoms do not worsen to an emergency-level threat. This can be done by taking medicines such as acetaminophen. Ask your doctor or local health department for further recommendations

  • If you notice any of these symptoms developing, you should seek emergency help immediately:

    • Trouble breathing

    • Constant chest pains

    • Inability to wake or stay awake

    • Bluish lips
  • Clean and disinfect areas that only you use such as the room you are isolating in and your bathroom

  • Have someone else in your house clean and disinfect common areas such as door handles, kitchen tops, and other surfaces that are commonly touched during the day

  • This cleaning should be done with household cleaners and disinfectants. Ensure you follow the instructions on the product for efficient usage
