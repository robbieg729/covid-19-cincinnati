Symptoms

People confirmed to have been infected have experienced a wide variety of symptoms, ranging from few symptoms or asymptomatic to severe illness, and in the worst cases, death. The most noticeable symptoms are:

  • Fever

  • Dry cough

  • Shortness of breath

  • Loss of smell or taste

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) say that symptoms can appear any time between 2 days and 14 days after exposure to the virus. Covid-19 can be fatal, especially in those with underlying health issues to do with the heart and lungs, and in those who are above 60 years of age.

If you begin to contract any of these symptoms, it is imperative that you take the necessary course of action immediately. See the 'What to do if you feel ill' tab under 'Guidance' for details on how to do so.
